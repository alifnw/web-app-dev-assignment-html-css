How To Buy a JDM Car from Japan

Everything you need to know about importing a vehicle to Indonesia — from auction bidding to Bea Cukai customs clearance and STNK registration.

The Import Process — Step by Step

1

Consultation & Car Selection

Tell us your dream car — make, model, year range, budget, and preferred condition. Our team will search Japanese auction houses (USS, HAA, JU, CAA) and present you with matching options including full auction inspection sheets.

Free consultation No commitment 24h response
2

Auction Bidding & Purchase

Once you approve a vehicle, we place bids on your behalf at Japanese auctions. Our agents have years of experience reading auction sheets and inspecting vehicles. Upon winning, we handle the purchase, deregistration (export cancellation/抹消), and arrange inland transport to the port.

Typical auction flow:

Bid → Win → Payment → Deregistration → Port Transport

3

Shipping (Japan → Indonesia)

The vehicle is shipped via RoRo (Roll-on/Roll-off) or container from Japanese ports (Yokohama, Nagoya, Kobe) to Indonesian ports (Tanjung Priok Jakarta, Tanjung Perak Surabaya). Shipping typically takes 10–18 days depending on route and vessel schedule.

4

Bea Cukai — Customs Clearance

This is the most critical step. Upon arrival at an Indonesian port, the vehicle must clear Direktorat Jenderal Bea dan Cukai (DJBC). We handle all customs documentation and duty payments on your behalf.

Import Duties & Taxes:

Bea Masuk (Import Duty)50% of CIF
PPN (VAT / Value Added Tax)11% of (CIF + Bea Masuk)
PPnBM (Luxury Goods Tax)15–125%*
PPh 22 (Income Tax)7.5% of (CIF + Bea Masuk)

*PPnBM rate depends on engine displacement and vehicle type per PMK regulations

CIF = Cost (car price) + Insurance + Freight (shipping)

Key Regulations:

  • •Vehicles must be brand new (CBU) OR imported under special category (returning citizen, diplomatic, or gift) per Permendag No. 36/2023
  • •Second-hand/used vehicle import requires API-U (Angka Pengenal Importir Umum) holder license
  • •Must pass emmission test (Uji Emisi) and roadworthiness inspection
  • •Wangan Imports ID holds all necessary import licenses — you don't need to worry about this
5

Vehicle Inspection & Type Approval

After customs clearance, the vehicle undergoes inspection by the Ministry of Transportation (Kementerian Perhubungan). This includes Uji Tipe (Type Approval), Uji Emisi (Emission Test), and VIN verification. We coordinate with authorized testing facilities to ensure your car passes all requirements.

6

Registration — STNK & BPKB

The final step is registering the vehicle at SAMSAT. We assist with obtaining the BPKB (vehicle ownership certificate), STNK (annual registration card), and license plates. Your car will be fully road-legal in Indonesia.
